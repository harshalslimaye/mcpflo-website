MCPFlo supports both light and dark themes, so the app can match your
system preference or personal choice.

## Switching themes

Toggle between light and dark mode from the app's theme control (typically
in the sidebar or settings area). The choice applies across the entire
app — sidebar, capability tree, forms, and response views all adapt
consistently.

## Why it matters

- **Reduced eye strain** during long testing sessions, especially in
  low-light environments.
- **Consistency with your OS/editor setup** — many developers prefer to
  keep all their tools in a matching theme.
- **Readability of responses** — syntax-highlighted JSON, code, and text
  render with appropriate contrast in both themes, so switching doesn't
  hurt legibility of the content you're inspecting.

## Persistence

Your theme choice is remembered across app restarts as part of your local
configuration.
